A definitive diagnosis is not a single event but a systematic process. It begins with the patient's story and progresses through specialized tests to pinpoint the precise cause.

Advanced Treatment Landscape

Treatment is highly specific to the diagnosis. For non-scarring alopecias, the goal is to halt progression and stimulate regrowth. For scarring alopecias, it is to stop inflammation and prevent permanent loss.

Condition Therapies Evidence & Notes
FPHL Topical Minoxidil (5%), Oral Spironolactone/Finasteride, PRP, LLLT, Microneedling Combination therapy is most effective. Emerging: Clascoterone (topical anti-androgen).
Telogen Effluvium Address trigger (stress, nutrition, illness), Stress Management, Nutritional Support Self-resolving in 6-12 months once trigger is managed. Minoxidil may speed recovery.
Alopecia Areata Corticosteroids (Injected/Topical), JAK Inhibitors (Baricitinib, Ritlecitinib) JAK inhibitors show >60% regrowth in severe cases. First-line for patchy AA is intralesional steroids.
Scarring Alopecias Corticosteroids, Hydroxychloroquine, Doxycycline, Dutasteride Early treatment is critical to halt irreversible follicular destruction.

Key Clinical Insights

An accurate diagnosis is paramount. The modern paradigm integrates clinical skill, trichoscopy, and lab work to form a precise diagnosis. Effective management requires a holistic plan that not only treats the scalp but also addresses the profound psychological impact of hair loss to ensure patient well-being.
